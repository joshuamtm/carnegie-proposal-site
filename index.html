Frequently Asked Questions

Addressing common concerns about AI adoption at Carnegie

How do we get resistant staff to start using AI?

We focus on low-risk, high-value "quick wins" like email drafting and meeting summaries. By starting with tasks that save time immediately, we build confidence gradually. Plus, with your President's performance review integration, we provide the support staff need to meet expectations comfortably.

We already have an AI Working Group. How do you fit in?

We enhance and support your existing structure. Your AI Working Group continues making governance decisions while we provide training, use case development, and implementation support. Think of us as extending your team's capacity, not replacing it.

What about our varying skill levels?

Our tiered approach addresses everyone: beginners get foundational skills and confidence, intermediate users develop department-specific applications, and advanced users become AI Champions who help their peers. Everyone progresses from their current level.

How do we identify what to use AI for?

This is a core focus. We run department-specific workshops to identify use cases, create a living library of Carnegie-specific applications, and facilitate peer sharing where your advanced users demonstrate their workflows. You'll leave with concrete, applicable examples.

Can we test this approach before committing fully?

Absolutely. Our Pilot Department option ($8,500) lets you test our approach with one team over 6 weeks. Or start with monthly advisory ($5,000/month) to explore options while you develop your strategy.

What makes you different from other AI trainers?

We're nonprofit-exclusive with 60+ years combined experience. We understand grant-making, program evaluation, and philanthropic workflows. Every example relates to your actual work—not generic business scenarios. Plus, we're building sustainable adoption, not consultant dependency.
